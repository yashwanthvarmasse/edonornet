Your Guide to Becoming a Donor

Learn about the process, requirements, and life-saving impact of donating blood through eDonorNet.

Why Your Donation Matters

Trauma & Surgery

Provides a critical lifeline to accident victims and patients undergoing major surgeries.

Chronic Illnesses

Essential for patients with conditions like sickle cell anemia or thalassemia.

Cancer Treatments

Helps replace blood cells lost during chemotherapy or radiation treatment.

Childbirth

Supports mothers who experience severe blood loss during or after childbirth.

The eDonorNet Donation Process

1. Register Online

Create your secure donor profile. Provide your blood type and basic medical history.

2. Get Matched

Receive alerts from eDonorNet when a patient nearby needs your blood type.

3. Donate Safely

Visit a certified partner blood bank or hospital. The process is safe and quick.

4. Save a Life

Your donation is processed and sent to the patient. You get an update on your impact!

Eligibility & Precautions

Donor Eligibility Requirements

  • Age: You must be between 18 and 65 years old.
  • Weight: You must weigh at least 50 kg (110 lbs).
  • Health: You must be in good general health.
  • Lifestyle: Wait 6 months after tattoos or piercings.
  • Cannot Donate if: You have a cold, flu, or infection.

Donation Day Precautions

Before Your Donation
  • Eat an iron-rich meal a few hours before.
  • Drink plenty of water (an extra 500ml).
  • Bring a valid photo ID.
After Your Donation
  • Rest and enjoy the provided snack and drink.
  • Avoid heavy lifting for the rest of the day.
  • Continue to hydrate well.
